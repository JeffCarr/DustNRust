Should we run our own infrastructure?

This isn't a recommendation, but a starting point for a conversation.

Everyone relies on Discord, Facebook Events, Eventbrite, Soundcloud, and other platforms they don't control. They're free and familiar, but they make decisions about our data, our community, and our reach. What would it look like to run our own tools instead, and is that worth it?

Honest tradeoffs // what we gain and lose

What we gain

  • Our data stays ours, no platform owns our community list
  • No algorithm deciding who sees our events
  • No risk of account bans or policy changes shutting us down
  • Role-based access, one platform, and the team, volunteers, and community each see what they need
  • One login across all tools, add or remove people cleanly
  • Everything on a single website for people to find events, purchase tickets, contact us, view past events, and coordinate
  • No ads, no tracking

What it costs us

  • Someone has to maintain it — this is ongoing, not a one-time job
  • New members have a steeper learning curve than Discord
  • Things break, and we fix them instead of filing a support ticket
  • Initial setup takes real time and effort
  • Server and service costs every month
  • The tools are often less polished than commercial alternatives
  • Getting people onto and using the new website instead of the websites they are familiar with

What it costs // rough numbers

Estimate, of course, actual costs depend on how much storage and traffic we use. Lower ticket fees (~$1 on a $25 ticket for Stripe) offsets this depending on volume.

~$20 per month Server hosting for the core stack
~$10 per month Email relay + domain registration
1–4 months Realistic time to stable customized setup

What we're not replacing // realistic limits

Some platforms are too embedded in how people find events to abandon entirely. Facebook Events and Instagram stay as outward-facing discovery tools, we're not trying to win people away from those. We'd use our own infrastructure for the people already in the community, and for the team running things.

Payment processing (Stripe) also stays external. There's no realistic self-hosted alternative for taking ticket money safely.

The question // what we're deciding

This isn't all-or-nothing. The minimum useful version — chat, file sharing, and ticketing — could be running soon. The radio and media archive can follow once the core is stable.

The real question is whether we have the time and desire to maintain this ongoing. The tools exist and they work. The bottleneck is desire and time, not technology.

If the answer is no, that's a valid answer. Staying on Discord costs nothing and requires nothing. This is worth doing if we want ownership and want to run it — not otherwise.
